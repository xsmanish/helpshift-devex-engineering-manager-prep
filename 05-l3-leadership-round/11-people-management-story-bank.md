# Story 1 — Addressing A Code Of Conduct Issue

## Question Variations

* Tell me about a difficult employee.
* Tell me about a conduct issue.
* Tell me about a time someone created conflict.
* How do you handle inappropriate behavior?

---

## Situation

A senior engineer was technically very strong and delivered consistently.

However, during architecture reviews and team discussions, he frequently interrupted others, dismissed ideas quickly, and occasionally made comments that team members perceived as disrespectful.

Over time, junior engineers became reluctant to participate in meetings.

---

## Task

Address the behavior without damaging morale, while maintaining team trust and psychological safety.

---

## Action

### Step 1

Collected facts.

Spoke with multiple participants.

Focused on observable behavior rather than opinions.

---

### Step 2

Conducted a private one-on-one discussion.

Used the SBI framework.

Situation:

Recent architecture reviews.

Behavior:

Interrupting team members and dismissing suggestions.

Impact:

Reduced participation and collaboration.

---

### Step 3

Made expectations clear.

Explained that leadership includes how results are achieved, not only technical outcomes.

---

### Step 4

Created improvement plan.

Focused on:

* Active listening
* Facilitating discussions
* Encouraging participation

---

### Step 5

Provided regular feedback after meetings.

---

## Result

Behavior improved significantly over the next two months.

Meeting participation increased.

Junior engineers became more engaged.

The engineer later became a strong mentor and technical leader.

---

## Leadership Lesson

High performance never excuses poor behavior.

Culture must be protected consistently.

# Story 2 — Improving Team Visibility

## Question Variations

* How do you improve team recognition?
* How do you increase visibility?
* How do you showcase team accomplishments?

---

## Situation

The platform team supported dozens of engineering teams.

Most successes were invisible.

Leadership only noticed platform teams when incidents occurred.

Morale began declining because engineers felt their contributions were not recognized.

---

## Task

Increase visibility of team impact and improve recognition.

---

## Action

Introduced monthly platform reviews.

Created dashboards showing:

* Deployment improvements
* Reliability metrics
* Cost savings
* Productivity gains

Encouraged engineers to present their work directly to leadership.

Highlighted individual contributions during leadership meetings.

Shared success stories across engineering channels.

---

## Result

Leadership awareness improved.

Engineers received more recognition.

Team morale increased.

Platform initiatives received stronger organizational support.

---

## Leadership Lesson

Good work does not automatically become visible.

Managers must create visibility mechanisms.

# Story 3 — Managing A Non-Performer

## Question Variations

* Tell me about underperformance.
* How do you improve weak performers?
* Tell me about a difficult performance discussion.

---

## Situation

An engineer who had previously performed well began missing commitments repeatedly.

Sprint commitments slipped.

Quality issues increased.

Stakeholders started raising concerns.

---

## Task

Identify the root cause and improve performance.

---

## Action

Scheduled a private discussion.

Focused on understanding rather than judgment.

Discovered the engineer was supporting multiple teams simultaneously and constantly context switching.

Created improvement plan:

* Reduced competing priorities
* Defined clear ownership
* Weekly checkpoints
* Better workload management

Provided support rather than pressure.

---

## Result

Performance improved within several sprints.

Stakeholder confidence returned.

The engineer remained a valuable contributor.

---

## Leadership Lesson

Many performance issues are organizational problems disguised as individual problems.

---

# Story 4 — Giving Difficult Feedback

## Question Variations

* How do you give negative feedback?
* Tell me about tough feedback.
* Tell me about a difficult conversation.

---

## Situation

A senior engineer consistently produced quality work but struggled in stakeholder interactions.

Cross-functional teams viewed him as difficult to work with.

---

## Task

Provide honest feedback while maintaining trust.

---

## Action

Used specific examples.

Avoided labels.

Focused on impact.

Explained:

"Your technical recommendations are strong, but the way they are communicated is creating resistance."

Discussed expectations.

Created growth plan around communication and influence.

---

## Result

Stakeholder relationships improved.

Engineer became more effective at influencing decisions.

Later led several cross-team initiatives successfully.

---

## Leadership Lesson

Feedback should focus on behavior and impact, not personality.

---

# Story 5 — Balancing Strong And Weak Performers

## Question Variations

* How do you manage different skill levels?
* How do you balance team capabilities?
* How do you prevent top performers from carrying everyone?

---

## Situation

The team included:

* Several senior engineers
* Mid-level engineers
* Newer engineers

Work distribution was becoming uneven.

Strong engineers were overloaded.

Junior engineers were underutilized.

---

## Task

Improve team balance while supporting growth.

---

## Action

Categorized work by complexity.

Assigned:

High complexity

→ Senior engineers

Medium complexity

→ Mid-level engineers

Growth opportunities

→ Junior engineers

Created mentoring partnerships.

Encouraged seniors to coach rather than rescue.

Provided stretch assignments.

---

## Result

Knowledge sharing improved.

Delivery became more balanced.

Junior engineers grew faster.

Senior engineers experienced less burnout.

---

## Leadership Lesson

Strong teams are built through capability development, not dependency on heroes.

---

# Story 6 — Promotion Discussion With An Unhappy Employee

## Question Variations

* Someone wanted promotion but wasn't ready.
* How do you handle disappointment?

---

## Situation

A high-performing engineer expected promotion.

Review process determined they were not yet operating consistently at the next level.

The engineer was frustrated.

---

## Task

Maintain trust while delivering difficult news.

---

## Action

Reviewed promotion criteria together.

Discussed:

* Strengths
* Gaps
* Examples
* Development opportunities

Created a six-month growth plan.

Provided opportunities to demonstrate next-level behaviors.

---

## Result

Engineer remained engaged.

Successfully achieved promotion later.

Relationship remained strong.

---

## Leadership Lesson

Promotion decisions should never be surprises.

# Story 7 — Resolving Team Morale Problems

## Question Variations

* Tell me about low morale.
* How do you improve engagement?

---

## Situation

Following several high-priority initiatives, team energy began declining.

Engineers appeared exhausted.

Participation dropped.

Engagement scores declined.

---

## Task

Restore morale and engagement.

---

## Action

Conducted listening sessions.

Identified concerns:

* Too many priorities
* Limited recognition
* Context switching

Implemented changes:

* Reduced work in progress
* Increased recognition
* Improved planning

Communicated priorities more clearly.

---

## Result

Morale improved.

Delivery quality improved.

Attrition concerns decreased.

---

## Leadership Lesson

Engagement improves when people feel heard and supported.

# Story 8 — Conflict Between Two Strong Engineers

## Question Variations

* Tell me about team conflict.
* How do you resolve disagreements?

---

## Situation

Two senior engineers disagreed strongly on architectural direction.

Discussions became increasingly personal.

Team productivity suffered.

---

## Task

Restore collaboration and reach decision.

---

## Action

Met individually.

Identified shared goals.

Facilitated structured architecture review.

Focused discussion on:

* Requirements
* Constraints
* Tradeoffs

Removed personalities from the conversation.

---

## Result

Decision reached.

Relationship improved.

Team confidence restored.

---

## Leadership Lesson

Conflict should focus on ideas, never individuals.

# Story 10 — Delegating Instead Of Doing

## Question Variations

* Tell me about your biggest leadership lesson.
* How did you transition from IC to manager?

---

## Situation

Early in management, I frequently solved technical problems myself because it felt faster.

Over time this created dependency.

---

## Task

Scale team effectiveness.

---

## Action

Shifted focus from solving problems personally to coaching others.

Delegated ownership.

Created accountability mechanisms.

Provided support without taking control.

---

## Result

Team became more autonomous.

Engineers grew faster.

Delivery scaled more effectively.

---

## Leadership Lesson

Managers scale through people.

Not through personal output.
