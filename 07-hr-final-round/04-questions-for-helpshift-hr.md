# 04 - Questions for Helpshift HR

> Helpshift Final HR Round Preparation
>
> One of the biggest mistakes candidates make in final HR discussions is not asking thoughtful questions.
>
> The questions you ask demonstrate:
>
> - What you value.
> - How you think.
> - Whether you are evaluating the company seriously.
> - Whether you are focused only on compensation or long-term success.
>
> Since this is the final HR round after successfully clearing the technical, leadership, and CTO discussions, the goal is to understand Helpshift as an employer and assess whether it is the right long-term fit.
>
> You do not need to ask all of these questions.
>
> Select the most relevant ones based on how the conversation flows.

---

# Section 1 - Employee Wellbeing

## Question

> Employee wellbeing has become increasingly important in high-performance engineering organizations. How does Helpshift support employee wellbeing beyond standard benefits?

### Why Ask This?

This demonstrates:

- Leadership maturity.
- Long-term thinking.
- Interest in sustainable performance.

---

## Question

> Are there specific initiatives or programs focused on maintaining healthy work-life balance across teams?

### Why Ask This?

Helps understand:

- Real culture.
- Expectations around workload.
- Burnout prevention practices.

---

## Question

> How does Helpshift measure employee engagement and overall employee satisfaction?

### Why Ask This?

Strong organizations actively measure employee sentiment.

Look for:

- Engagement surveys.
- Action plans.
- Leadership participation.

# Questions To Avoid

Avoid questions that can be easily answered by the company website.

Examples:

❌ What does Helpshift do?

❌ How many employees does Helpshift have?

❌ Where are your offices located?

---

Avoid overly negative questions:

❌ How often do people leave?

❌ Is attrition a problem?

❌ How many layoffs have happened?

---

Avoid questions that focus only on benefits:

❌ How many holidays?

❌ How many leaves?

❌ When is appraisal?

These can be discussed later if needed.

---

# Closing Question (Recommended)

End with:

> Based on our conversations and feedback from the interview process, is there anything else you would like me to clarify or elaborate on?

### Why This Is Powerful

It:

- Demonstrates openness.
- Shows confidence.
- Allows HR to surface concerns.
- Gives you one final opportunity to strengthen your candidacy.

---

# Key Message HR Should Remember

> I am evaluating Helpshift not only as my next role but as a place where I can contribute, grow, and build long-term relationships. Understanding the culture, people, and employee experience is just as important to me as understanding the role itself.
