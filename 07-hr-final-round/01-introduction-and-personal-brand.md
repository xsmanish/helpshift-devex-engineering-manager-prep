# 5 Minute Leadership Introduction

I have spent more than 15 years in technology, with most of my recent experience focused on Platform Engineering, Cloud Infrastructure, SRE, DevOps, and Engineering Leadership.

One theme that has remained consistent throughout my career is my interest in building systems that scale—not only technology systems but also teams, processes, and organizational capabilities.

Earlier in my career, I was primarily focused on solving technical challenges. I enjoyed designing systems, automating infrastructure, improving reliability, and building engineering solutions.

As my responsibilities grew, I realized that technology problems are often easier to solve than people and organizational challenges.

That realization shifted my focus toward leadership.

Today, I see my role as helping engineering teams succeed by creating clarity, removing obstacles, building trust, and aligning engineering efforts with business goals.

Some of the initiatives I am most proud of are not necessarily the most technically complex. They are the initiatives that created long-term value for teams and organizations, such as:

- Platform standardization initiatives.
- Developer productivity improvements.
- Governance automation.
- Cloud cost optimization programs.
- Engineering process improvements.

Equally important to me is helping people grow.

Over the years, I have mentored engineers, supported team members through challenges, helped people prepare for new opportunities, and worked to create environments where individuals feel trusted and empowered.

Outside work, I enjoy mentoring aspiring engineers and helping job seekers prepare for careers in technology. I also regularly visit ISKON and draw inspiration from the principles of humility, accountability, and focusing on actions rather than outcomes.

I believe these personal values have significantly influenced my leadership style.

Ultimately, I believe strong engineering organizations are built when technical excellence, business outcomes, and people development are all given equal importance.

That balance is what I strive to bring as a leader.
