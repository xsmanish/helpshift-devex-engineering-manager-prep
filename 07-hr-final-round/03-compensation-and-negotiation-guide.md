# 03 - Compensation and Negotiation Guide

> Helpshift Final HR Round Preparation
>
> The purpose of compensation discussions is not to maximize salary at any cost.
>
> The goal is to achieve a fair outcome that reflects:
>
> - Experience
> - Leadership capability
> - Market value
> - Organizational impact
> - Long-term growth opportunity
>
> A successful negotiation is one where both parties feel comfortable with the outcome.

---

# Compensation Discussion Mindset

Before discussing numbers, remember:

The company is not only hiring your skills.

They are hiring:

- Experience
- Leadership capability
- Judgment
- Team building ability
- Stakeholder management
- Organizational impact

The conversation should remain professional and collaborative.

Avoid treating it as a bargaining exercise.

Instead, approach it as a discussion about mutual value.

# If HR Asks

## What Is Your Current Compensation?

Provide a clear and factual answer.

Example:

> My current total compensation consists of fixed compensation, performance-linked variable components, and benefits. I would be happy to discuss the complete compensation structure in detail.

Always provide accurate information.

Never inflate numbers.

---

# If HR Asks

## What Is Your Expected Compensation?

Recommended Answer

> I am looking for a compensation package that reflects the scope of the role, my experience, leadership responsibilities, and market standards. I am open to discussing the overall package and would like to understand Helpshift's compensation structure before finalizing expectations.

---

# If They Push For A Number

Recommended Approach

Provide a range rather than a single number.

Example:

> Based on my experience, leadership responsibilities, and market benchmarks, I am targeting a total compensation range that appropriately reflects the level of impact expected in this role. However, I am flexible and would prefer to evaluate the complete package, including growth opportunities and benefits.

# What Not To Say

Avoid:

### "I Need The Highest Offer Possible"

This signals a transactional mindset.

---

### "I Have Multiple Offers So Please Match"

Unless absolutely necessary.

---

### "Salary Is My Only Priority"

Leadership candidates are expected to evaluate broader factors.

---

### "I Will Join Only If..."

Avoid rigid ultimatums.

---

# If The Offer Is Lower Than Expected

Recommended Response

> Thank you for sharing the offer details. I am genuinely excited about the opportunity and the conversations I have had throughout the process. I would appreciate some time to review the complete package and discuss whether there is flexibility to better align the offer with my experience and the scope of the role.

---

Show enthusiasm.

---

Step 2

---

Step 3

---

Step 4

---

Step 5

# Joining Bonus Discussion

If there is a significant gap between expectations and the offer:

Example:

> If there are constraints around the fixed compensation component, would Helpshift be open to exploring a joining bonus or other compensation mechanisms to help bridge the gap?

This is often easier for companies to approve.

---

# Notice Period Discussion

Likely Question

## What Is Your Notice Period?

Suggested Answer

> My current notice period is 30 days. I will work professionally with my current organization to ensure a smooth transition while also making every effort to join Helpshift as quickly as possible.
