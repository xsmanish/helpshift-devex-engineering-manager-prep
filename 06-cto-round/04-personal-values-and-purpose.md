# 04 - Personal Values and Purpose

> CTO Interview Preparation
>
> Over the years, I have come to realize that leadership is shaped not only by professional experiences but also by personal values.
>
> Technology, platforms, delivery, and business outcomes are important, but the principles that guide how we treat people, make decisions, and respond to challenges often come from experiences outside the workplace.
>
> The purpose of this document is to articulate the personal values that influence my leadership style and decision-making approach.

---

# Who Am I Beyond Work?

Professionally, I have spent more than 15 years building and operating technology platforms, cloud infrastructure, and engineering systems.

However, beyond technology, I see myself as someone who values:

- Continuous learning
- Personal growth
- Humility
- Service to others
- Meaningful relationships
- Helping people succeed

While I enjoy solving technical challenges, I find the greatest satisfaction in helping people grow and achieve things they once thought were beyond their reach.

---

# The Role of Spirituality in My Life

One important influence in my life is spirituality.

I regularly visit ISKON and draw inspiration from the teachings of Krishna.

For me, spirituality is not about rituals alone.

It is about perspective.

It helps me remain grounded during both success and failure.

---

## Lessons That Influence My Leadership

### Focus on Actions, Not Outcomes

One lesson that resonates deeply with me is the importance of focusing on effort, preparation, and responsibility rather than becoming overly attached to outcomes.

As a leader, this means:

- Making thoughtful decisions.
- Taking ownership.
- Preparing teams for success.
- Accepting uncertainty when outcomes cannot be fully controlled.

---

### Stay Calm During Uncertainty

Engineering leadership often involves:

- Production incidents.
- Organizational change.
- Conflicting priorities.
- High-pressure situations.

Spiritual practice has helped me remain calm and objective during stressful situations.

I have learned that teams look to leaders for stability when things become uncertain.

---

### Practice Humility

No leader has all the answers.

I strongly believe that good ideas can come from:

- Senior engineers.
- Junior engineers.
- Product managers.
- Stakeholders.
- Customers.

Humility creates space for learning and collaboration.

---

### Treat People with Respect

Every individual is dealing with challenges that may not be visible.

This belief influences how I interact with people both professionally and personally.

---

# Giving Back Through Mentorship

One activity that has been particularly meaningful to me is helping people enter and grow within the technology industry.

Over the years, I have voluntarily:

- Conducted mock interviews.
- Reviewed resumes.
- Guided aspiring engineers.
- Shared practical industry knowledge.
- Helped professionals prepare for career transitions.

This was never part of a formal responsibility.

It was simply something I enjoyed doing.

---

## Why It Matters to Me

I remember how valuable guidance was during different stages of my own career.

Many talented people struggle not because they lack capability, but because they lack direction, confidence, or access to mentorship.

Helping someone overcome those barriers can have a lasting impact on their life and career.

---

## Leadership Lesson

> Leadership is not measured by how many people work for you. It is measured by how many people grow because of your influence.

# Lessons Learned from Growing Up with Pets

I do not currently have pets because of the responsibilities that came with relocating for education and career opportunities.

However, growing up with pets taught me several lessons that still resonate today.

---

## Responsibility

Caring for another living being requires consistency and commitment.

Leadership is similar.

People depend on leaders to provide support, guidance, and stability.

---

## Patience

Progress is rarely immediate.

Trust, growth, and learning take time.

This lesson applies equally to engineering teams and personal relationships.

---

## Care and Attention

Small actions often have a significant impact.

Checking in with people, listening actively, and being present can make a meaningful difference.

# What Motivates Me Today?

At this stage of my career, my motivation has evolved.

Earlier in my career, success was often defined by:

- Technical achievements.
- Promotions.
- Certifications.
- Large projects.

Today, success feels different.

---

## Building Strong Teams

I enjoy helping teams become more capable, confident, and effective.

---

## Developing Future Leaders

One of the most rewarding experiences is seeing someone grow into responsibilities they once believed were beyond their reach.

---

## Creating Lasting Impact

I am increasingly motivated by initiatives that create long-term value for organizations and people.

Examples include:

- Platform engineering.
- Developer productivity.
- Engineering culture.
- Leadership development.
- Organizational effectiveness.

---

## Continuous Learning

I remain curious.

Technology evolves rapidly, and I enjoy learning new ideas, approaches, and perspectives.

---

# My Leadership Philosophy

Over the years, my leadership philosophy has become relatively simple:

---

## Lead with Empathy

Understand people before judging performance.

---

## Lead with Clarity

Provide context, direction, and transparency.

---

## Lead with Accountability

Support people while maintaining high standards.

---

## Lead with Humility

Stay open to learning regardless of title or experience.

---

## Lead Through Service

The role of leadership is not to be served.

The role of leadership is to help others succeed.

---

# What Kind of Legacy Do I Want to Leave?

If people remember me years after we have worked together, I hope it is not because of a particular project or technology initiative.

I hope they remember:

- That I treated them fairly.
- That I helped them grow.
- That I listened.
- That I supported them during difficult moments.
- That I challenged them to become better versions of themselves.

To me, that is the most meaningful form of leadership.

# CTO Conversation Talking Points

If asked:

### "What drives you personally?"

You can answer:

> What motivates me most today is helping people grow while solving meaningful business problems. Technology is something I enjoy, but building strong teams, developing future leaders, and creating long-term impact is what keeps me engaged and excited.

---

### "How has spirituality influenced your leadership?"

You can answer:

> Spirituality has helped me remain grounded and focused on what truly matters. It has taught me the importance of humility, respect, accountability, and staying calm during uncertainty. These principles have shaped how I lead teams and make decisions.

---

### "Why do you spend time mentoring others?"

You can answer:

> I have always believed that opportunities multiply when knowledge is shared. Throughout my career, I have benefited from people who invested time in helping me grow. Mentoring others is one way I can give back and help someone accelerate their own journey.

---

### "What is most important to you as a leader?"

You can answer:

> Delivering results is important, but I believe the best leaders create environments where people can thrive. If the team succeeds, grows, and feels supported while achieving meaningful outcomes, I consider that a successful leadership experience.

---

# Key Message for the CTO

> My leadership style is shaped by a combination of professional experience and personal values. I believe strong organizations are built by people who lead with empathy, humility, accountability, and purpose. Technology enables business success, but people ultimately create it. My goal has always been to build environments where both can thrive together.
