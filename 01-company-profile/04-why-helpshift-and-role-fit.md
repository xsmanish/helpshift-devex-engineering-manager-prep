## Personal Connection to Helpshift

### Why Helpshift Specifically?

Helpshift represents a unique convergence of product, engineering, and platform challenges that are ideal for a Developer Productivity leader:

1. **Platform DNA:** Helpshift's core product *is* a platform (the SDK). They understand platform thinking at their core. This is not a company where you need to convince leadership that platforms matter — they already know.

2. **Engineering maturity:** A 15-year-old company with a co-founder CTO who is a deeply respected technologist. This is an engineering organisation that values technical excellence, long-term thinking, and quality — attributes that matter deeply to a successful platform engineering practice.

3. **The Pune connection:** The primary R&D centre in Pune, India, means significant engineering ownership, autonomy, and impact sit here. For someone based in India, this is a rare opportunity to lead a strategic function with global impact without needing to relocate.

4. **Capital-efficient execution:** Helpshift has grown sustainably. This means there's real discipline — platform investments need to demonstrate ROI. This aligns with my approach: platform engineering must be grounded in business value, not just cool technology.

5. **AI inflection point:** AI is transforming customer service. Helpshift needs its platform to support rapid AI experimentation, model serving, and data pipelines. This is a greenfield opportunity to build the ML platform layer.

### Why This Role, Why Now?

- **Platform maturity transition:** Helpshift is moving from a proactive platform team to a self-service, product-oriented platform organisation. This is exactly the transition I have experience leading.
- **Scaling challenge:** As the company grows its customer base and AI capabilities, the developer experience and infrastructure must scale accordingly.
- **Foundational work:** This is a build role, not a maintenance role. The opportunity to shape the platform vision, team, and culture from a position of influence.

## Questions for the Interviewer

### Strategic Questions (for Hiring Manager / CTO)

1. **"How do you currently measure developer productivity at Helpshift? Are there DORA or similar metrics being tracked?"**
   - *Reveals:* How mature their DevEx measurement is. What they already know.

2. **"What is the biggest friction point your engineers face today in their daily workflow?"**
   - *Reveals:* Whether they have identified the real pain points. What's most urgent.

3. **"How is the platform team currently structured, and where does this role fit in the org chart?"**
   - *Reveals:* Reporting lines, scope, and influence of the role.

4. **"What does success look like for this role in the first 6 months and first 12 months?"**
   - *Reveals:* Their expectations, priorities, and whether they have a clear vision.

5. **"How do you see AI/ML changing Helpshift's platform infrastructure needs in the next 1–2 years?"**
   - *Reveals:* Their forward-thinking about ML platform, model serving, etc.

6. **"What is the relationship like between the Pune and SF engineering teams?"**
   - *Reveals:* Collaboration dynamics, autonomy, and potential challenges.

7. **"How is platform adoption currently measured and incentivised across teams?"**
   - *Reveals:* Whether platform is a choice or a mandate. How they drive adoption.

8. **"What's the most important thing the platform team has delivered recently, and what's the biggest miss?"**
   - *Reveals:* Their self-awareness. What they're proud of and what they regret.

### Cultural Questions

9. **"How would you describe the engineering culture in three words?"**
   - *Reveals:* Whether it matches your working style.

10. **"What is the on-call experience like for engineers today?"**
    - *Reveals:* Toil levels, SRE maturity, and work-life balance.

11. **"How are technical decisions made and documented at Helpshift?"**
    - *Reveals:* Their approach to architecture, ADRs, consensus-building.

12. **"What's the one thing you'd change about engineering at Helpshift if you had unlimited resources?"**
    - *Reveals:* Leadership's aspirations and frustrations.

## Suggested Answers Framework

### Q: "Why do you want to work at Helpshift?"

**Answer Structure:**

> "There are three reasons that make Helpshift a compelling opportunity for me:

> **First, [Platform DNA].** Helpshift's core product is a platform. You've been building developer-facing SDKs for over a decade. This means there's a deep organisational understanding of what it takes to build platforms that developers love — from API design to versioning to documentation. That cultural foundation is rare, and it's exactly where platform engineering thrives.

> **Second, [The Inflection Point].** Helpshift is at a strategic inflection point — scaling engineering, investing in AI, and becoming more platform-driven. The decision to hire a Developer Productivity Manager is a signal that you're ready to invest in the developer experience as a competitive advantage. I've been through this transition before, and I know what it takes to make it successful.

> **Third, [The Impact Opportunity].** With 100–150 engineers across Pune and San Francisco, there's enormous leverage in getting the platform right. Every improvement in developer productivity — faster CI/CD, better developer environments, self-service infrastructure — compounds across the entire engineering organisation. The impact is real, measurable, and business-critical.

> Additionally, the fact that your CTO is a respected technologist and the Pune office has high engineering ownership makes this an ideal environment for a platform engineering leader to make a lasting impact."

### Q: "Who are our competitors and how should we differentiate?"

**Answer Structure:**

> "Your primary competition is from generalist customer service platforms — Zendesk and Intercom, which have broader market reach and larger sales teams, and emerging AI-focused entrants like Ada.

> However, your differentiation is clear:

> 1. **In-app SDK depth:** No one has spent 10+ years perfecting the in-app mobile SDK experience. This is a moat.
> 2. **Gaming vertical:** Supercell, Zynga etc. are the most demanding customers possible. The fact that you serve them at scale is your best reference.
> 3. **AI automation maturity:** You've been doing AI-powered automation since before it was trendy. Your data advantage in training automation models is significant.

> From a platform perspective, the way to maintain this differentiation is to make it *easy* for developers to integrate and customise Helpshift. If your SDK adoption is frictionless and your APIs are best-in-class, developers will choose you over competitors — even if their procurement team leans toward a bigger vendor."
